Sentiment Analysis of Ukrainian Conflict Via Twitter

The data you will see on this site is representative of more than 2,700,000 tweets collected from February 27th, through March 2nd, following the February 24th Russian invasion of Ukraine. The aim of this analysis is to produce insight on how English speaking Twitter users engage with a war that is globally visible in ways that previous military conflicts have not yet been accessible. While the original intention of this review was to compare the sentiments of stratified socio-political groups in the United States, the findings were similar enough that it begged a few questions of a more universal sentimentality on the issue: "Does political stratification in the United States influence the ways its citizens engage with outbreak of war, or are the sentiments of war a common expression of shared humanity?
